FAQs

What types of job opportunities do you offer in the NDIS sector?

We offer a range of job opportunities, including support workers, allied health professionals, administrative roles, and management positions within the NDIS sector.

What qualifications are required to work in the NDIS?

Qualifications vary depending on the role. Support workers typically need a Certificate III or IV in Individual Support, while allied health professionals require relevant degrees and registrations.

Do I need an NDIS Worker Screening Check to apply?

Yes, most NDIS roles require an NDIS Worker Screening Check and a Working with Children Check (WWCC) to ensure the safety and well-being of participants.

How do you match candidates with the right NDIS employers?

We assess your qualifications, experience, and preferences to match you with suitable employers in the NDIS sector, ensuring a good fit for both job seekers and providers.

Do you offer training or upskilling programs for NDIS job seekers?

Yes, we provide training and upskilling opportunities, including first aid certification, disability awareness, and specialized NDIS training to enhance your employability.
